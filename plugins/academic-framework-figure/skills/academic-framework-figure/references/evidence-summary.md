# Evidence summary

The bundled `paper-style-evidence.json` contains 60 reviewed records across 15 venue-year groups:

- 20 computer-vision papers;
- 20 general machine-learning papers;
- 20 language, multimodal, graphics, and broader computing papers.

The sample encodes four records per venue-year and records abstract/method/caption inspection, layout, branch and panel counts, raster share, palette size, typography class, icon density, connector routing, training/inference distinctions, shared-parameter expression, and profile assignment.

The strongest cross-domain patterns were:

- low-complexity methods benefit from a small number of visually consistent modules and whitespace;
- image-heavy methods are clearest when examples remain aligned with their modality lane and color;
- multi-loss and agent/training pipelines need panels, named ports, and a dedicated convergence column;
- shared parameters are easier to understand as one module with multiple ports than duplicated networks plus a “shared weights” annotation;
- thumbnails reveal hierarchy failures that are not obvious at editing zoom.

These are coded design observations, not claims that every paper follows the same house style.
